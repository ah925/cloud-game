Cirrus 巻雲 (Kōun)

×

Characteristics

Thin, wispy clouds made of ice crystals that look like delicate, feathery strands high in the sky.

Altitude

High clouds form above 18,000 feet (5,500 meters).

Weather Indications

Although they usually signify fair weather, sometimes it can be an indicator of an approaching weather front.

Cirrostratus 巻層雲 (Kōsōun)

×

Characteristics

Transparent, whitish cloud veil that often covers the entire sky. These clouds can create halos around the sun or moon.

Altitude

High clouds form above 18,000 feet (5,500 meters).

Weather Indications

Can signify the possibility of approaching precipitation within 24-36 hours.

Cirrocumulus 巻積雲 (Kōsekiun)

×

Characteristics

Small, rounded white puffs that appear in long rows. They don't appear often so if you see it you are quite lucky! They create a rippled appearance sometimes called a "mackerel sky."

Altitude

High clouds form above 18,000 feet (5,500 meters).

Weather Indications

Signifies fair but cold weather.

Altostratus 高層雲 (Kōsōun)

×

Characteristics

Gray or blue-gray sheet-like clouds that cover the sky. The sun may be dimly visible through these clouds, creating a frosted glass effect.

Altitude

Middle clouds form between 6,500 and 18,000 feet (2,000-5,500 meters).

Weather Indications

Is usually seen before a warm front and continuous precipitation.

Altocumulus 高積雲 (Kōsekiun)

×

Characteristics

White or gray patch-like clouds with a rolled appearance, often arranged in waves or bands. These clouds are composed of water droplets.

Altitude

Middle clouds form between 6,500 and 18,000 feet (2,000-5,500 meters).

Weather Indications

Signifies instability in the atmosphere and potential thunderstorm development later in the day.

Stratus 層雲 (Sōun)

×

Characteristics

Gray cloud layer resembling fog that doesn't reach the ground. These clouds can produce drizzle or light snow.

Altitude

Low clouds form below 6,500 feet (2,000 meters).

Weather Indications

Usually indicates overcast conditions with occasional light precipitation.

Stratocumulus 層積雲 (Sōsekiun)

×

Characteristics

Puffy white/gray clouds arranged in a low layer or patch with some spots of clear sky visible. One of the most common cloud types.

Altitude

Low clouds form below 6,500 feet (2,000 meters).

Weather Indications

Signifies stable conditions with a small chance of light precipitation.

Nimbostratus 乱層雲 (Ransōun)

×

Characteristics

Dark gray cloud layer that usually covers the entire sky and produces constant rain or snow. These thick clouds often block out the sun completely.

Altitude

Low to middle clouds, typically below 10,000 feet (3,000 meters).

Weather Indications

Usually signify continuous moderate to heavy precipitation.

Cumulus 積雲 (Sekiun)

×

Characteristics

Fluffy clouds with flat bases and rounded tops, resembling a cotton ball. They form due to thermal convection.

Altitude

Low to middle clouds, typically between 1,000-6,500 feet (300-2,000 meters).

Weather Indications

Usually signify fair weather but it is possible that may grow vertically and develop into thunderstorms.

Cumulonimbus 積乱雲 (Sekiranun)

×

Characteristics

Massive thunderstorm clouds with a dark base and tall, flat tops. These clouds often result in heavy rain, hail, lightning, and even tornadoes.

Altitude

Vertical development from low to high altitudes, typically from 1,000 feet up to 50,000+ feet (300-15,000+ meters).

Weather Indications

Usually signify severe weather, including heavy precipitation, lightning, strong winds, and sometimes hail or tornadoes.
